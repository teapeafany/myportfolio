00: Defining the Problem

Context

It started with a simple question:
why do so many of our peers struggle to cook for themselves?
During the transition into college life, we noticed how cooking, something that should feel comforting, often became intimidating or forgotten. Together with fellow designer Anieka, we set out to understand that gap.

Project Overview

Chow Club grew from those conversations. It is a gamified social platform designed to make cooking approachable, playful, and confidence-building for young adults. By turning everyday meals into moments of creativity and connection, we aimed to reframe cooking not as a chore but as a story worth savoring.

01: Gathering Insights

Insights

From user interviews and peer discussions, we discovered that most students:

  • Struggle to find motivation to cook for one.
  • Feel overwhelmed by recipes with complex steps.
  • Miss the sense of community around cooking.

How might we motivate struggling
college students to build healthy
and responsible eating habits?

02: Design Ideation

Direction

We wanted to make cooking feel more like a game, encouraging progress through small, achievable steps. This meant introducing challenges, rewards, and community interaction.

Ideation

We began by sketching out low-fidelity wireframes inspired by mobile gaming interfaces, focusing on bright visuals, progress tracking, and minimal friction in onboarding.

03: Final Iterations

Minor Gamification

Keeping it simple, the user can complete recipes to level up and unlock more recipes. This way, gamification is meant to act as motivation rather than be the central goal.


Step-By-Step User Guide

Users can interact while cooking without a need to read through a long recipe list.


Social Motivation

Most users find motivation through social interaction and friends. Now, friends can learn recipes together and share their completed dishes in their profile as well as on other social media platforms.
