00: Project Overview

Context

Science Olympiad competitions involve complex scheduling and multiple teams, making management a logistical challenge. They needed a system that could simplify responsibilities for admins and coaches.

The Challenge

Scioly is a scoring and tournament management system used to run Science Olympiad competitions at Georgia Tech. I was tasked with designing new role-based features that streamlined event scheduling for admins and made team registration intuitive for coaches.

How might we streamline tournament management for Science Olympiad competitions at Georgia Tech?

01: Design Process

User Research

Conducted interviews with tournament administrators and coaches to understand pain points in the current system. Key findings included confusion around registration deadlines and difficulty tracking team availability.

Wireframing

Created low-fidelity wireframes focusing on role-based access and intuitive navigation. Prioritized clear visual hierarchy and minimal cognitive load for both admin and coach users.

Prototyping

Developed interactive prototypes to test user flows and gather feedback on the scheduling interface. Iterated based on usability testing with actual tournament organizers.

02: My Contributions

Coach Role Interface

Designed a clear login and dashboard where coaches can view events, browse available time slots, and self-register their teams. Reduced confusion and reliance on manual admin updates.

Admin Role Flow

Prototyped the scheduling system for generating event time blocks, setting maximum slots per event, and auto-populating them into the coach dashboard.

Interaction Design

Created detailed interaction states (hover indicators, selection confirmations, and real-time availability tracking) to make time slot selection intuitive and error-free.
