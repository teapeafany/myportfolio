00: Project Overview

Overview

Echosphere is a conceptual music-discovery experience we developed, targeted towards all music listeners ages 18-24, focused on breaking algorithmic filter bubbles and introducing a genuine exploratory experience.

The Context

Current music platforms like Spotify and Apple Music overwhelmingly rely on recommendation algorithms that recycle familiar content, limiting genuine discovery and weakening emotional engagement with music. Users reported feeling disconnected from artists, overwhelmed by surface-level recommendations, and constrained by systems that rarely reflect their mood, headspace, or social environment.

The Problems

  • Over-reliance on algorithms means repetitive, predictable recommendations, limiting genuine discovery.
  • Users feel emotionally disconnected from artists when discovering them on streaming platforms, as content lacks depth, storytelling, or personal context.
  • Users are frustrated when music platforms are context blind, ignoring mood, environment, and social space.

Our Goals

  • Break out of algorithmic filter bubbles
  • Rebuild meaningful artist-listener relationships
  • Enable emotionally-intelligent, context-aware music discovery

How might we create a music discovery experience that breaks algorithmic filter bubbles and rebuilds meaningful connections between artists and listeners?

01: Design Process

Research Analysis: Affinity Mapping

Through user interviews and affinity mapping, we identified key themes in how users discover and connect with music. Our analysis revealed patterns around emotional connection, discovery and curation, contextual and emotional music preferences, social aspects of music sharing, artist-listener relationships, and the limitations of algorithmic recommendations.

Design Goals

Our user research revealed a clear pattern: current music platforms are failing to create meaningful, emotional, and socially rich discovery experiences. To address these gaps, our design goals focus on three core areas:

  • Enable Human-Centered, Non-Algorithmic Social Discovery
  • Deepen Artist-Listener Relationships
  • Create Emotionally Intelligent Music Discovery

02: Our Design Features

Feature: Community Discovery Spaces

Instead of AI-generated suggestions, users can explore the music spheres of people around them, friends, peers, or even strangers nearby. This supports authentic discovery through human taste and shared moments, fulfilling users' need for subtle, meaningful social connection without overwhelming feeds or performative social pressure.

Design Goal: Enable Human-Centered, Non-Algorithmic Social Discovery

Feature: User Spheres

User Spheres create immersive, story-driven spaces crafted by artists and friends. By offering behind-the-scenes content, personal updates, and curated music bubbles that they can share with their fans and friends, this feature directly responds to users' desire for deeper context and stronger emotional ties with the artists they follow.

Design Goal: Deepen Artist-Listener Relationships

Feature: Mood & Environment Spheres

Users reported that algorithmic playlists feel shallow and disconnected from their actual mood or environment. Mood & Environment Spheres use contextual cues (weather, time, location, and listening habits) to generate more personal, emotionally resonant discovery paths. These spheres adapt through interactive 'Vibe checks,' giving users control over how their exploration evolves.

Design Goal: Create Emotionally Intelligent Music Discovery
